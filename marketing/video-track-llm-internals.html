AWESOME AI LEARNING · INSIDE THE LLM

You type. It answers. What happens in between?

Follow one message through the inside of a large language model.

It is not looking anything up.

There is no database of answers inside. The model predicts the next token, and a few key ideas make that work.

STEP 1 · TOKENIZER

Text becomes tokens.

The model reads tokens, not characters. Common words stay whole; rare words get split into pieces.

INPUT
Transformers are unbelievable
TOKENS
Transformersareunbelievable
STEP 2 · EMBEDDING

Tokens become vectors.

Computers only understand numbers. Words with similar meanings sit close together, so similarity can be calculated.

MEANING SPACE
catkittendog carbustrain
close together = similar meaning
STEP 3 · ATTENTION

Every word sees the whole sentence.

For a word like “it”, the meaning depends on context. Attention scores how much each word should attend to the others.

ATTENTION
Thecatsatbecauseitwastired
“it” attends to “cat”
STEP 4 · PREDICTION

It guesses the next token.

At every step the model scores a whole row of candidates. One token after another, an answer grows.

STEP 5 · KNOWLEDGE

What it knows is baked in.

Pretraining compresses knowledge into the parameters. Anything after the cutoff, or private to you, it simply does not know.

Trained once. Run every time.

Every chat is one inference, inside a fixed context window. Go over the limit and the earliest tokens fall out.
